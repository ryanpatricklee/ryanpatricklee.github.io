New York Citi Bike Analysis

Overview

NY Citi Bike is a bicycle sharing system operating in New York City. It was launched in May 2013 and is one of the largest bike sharing programs in the United States. Over the years, NY Citi Bike has expanded its coverage area and the number of bikes available. It operates through a network of docking stations where users can pick up and drop off bikes. These stations are located throughout Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens.

Goal

To better understand the behavior of NY Citi Bike's customer base (subscribers and customers) and how they use NY Citi Bikes.

Purpose & Context

This project was part of my data analytics course at CareerFoundry to showcase the technical skills needed to analyze data using Python and the soft skills needed to communicate insights using Tableau.

201903-citibike-tripdata (Source: NY Citi Bike)

Analysis

  • Wrote a python script to conduct a geospatial analysis. This involved using a .geojson file to help create a choropleth map using the folium library.
  • Wrote a python script to conduct a linear regression analysis.

Preparing for Influenza in the United States

Overview

The United States has an influenza season where more people than usual suffer from the flu. Some people, particularly those in vulnerable populations, develop serious complications and end up in the hospital. Hospitals and clinics need additional staff to adequately treat these extra patients. A medical staffing agency provides this temporary staff.

Goal

Help the medical staffing agency that provides temporary workers to clinics and hospitals on an as- needed basis by determining when to send staff, and how many, to each state, with the following criteria:

  • A staffing plan that utilizes all available agency staff per state requirements, without necessitating additional resources.
  • Minimal instances of staff-to- patient ratio below 90% and above 110%.

Purpose & Context

This project was part of my data analytics course at CareerFoundry to showcase the technical skills needed to analyze data using Excel and the soft skills needed to communicate insights.

Data Sets Used

  • Influenza deaths by geography, time, age, and gender (Source: CDC)
    Download
  • Population data by geography (Source: US Census Bureau)
    Download

Data Preparation

  • Created a list of data questions that needs answering for analysis.
  • Designed a data research project and formulated a research hypothesis.
  • Looked at the data set to understand the source, coverage, relevance, and limitations, ensuring it aligns with the business requirements.
  • Created a data profile for each of the data sets to be used in analysis, including information on data types, data integrity issues (accuracy and consistency), any cleaning that was conducted, as well as summary statistics in each profile.
  • Implemented additional data quality measures to data profiles related to completeness, uniqueness, and timeliness.

Analysis

  • Integrated data from two sources into one cohesive data set by aggregating the data, applying data transformations, and using VLOOKUP( ).
  • Normalized the influenza death count for each state to obtain the mortality rate for each state.
  • Identified a relationship between population size and influenza deaths and checked for correlation.
  • Formulated a statistical hypothesis regarding age and influenza deaths.
  • Tested the hypothesis by performing a statistical test and interpreting the results.
  • Created an interim report consolidating the findings of the analysis.
  • Used Tableau to create visualizations to construct insights for strategically allocating additional medical staff for each state throughout the year.

Insights and Data Visualizations

Who is impacted most by influenza?

From 2009 to 2017, more than 40,000 people in the United States die from influenza every year. While every age group is impacted by influenza, the older populations (people 65 years or older) are impacted the most. The data shows at least 88% of influenza deaths in the United States were people 65 years or older.

When is influenza season in the United States?

Death from influenza happens all year round, but there is a seasonal trend across the four regions in the United States. This seasonal trend allowed for an accurate forecast for the year 2018.

Which states need more assistance?

The majority of influenza deaths are people 65 years or older. Consequently, states with a higher population count tend to have more people 65 years or older, and therefore, are accompanied with a higher influenza death count.

Recommendations

Since influenza occurs all-year-round in the United States, temporary staff should be sent all-year-round. The amount of staff sent to each state should be adjusted on a monthly basis depending on the need of each state.

  • High-need states: California, Florida, New York, Texas
  • Medium-need states: Washington, Illinois, Ohio, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, Michigan
  • Low-need states: The rest of the United States not mentioned above

During the peak season of influenza, 75% of staff should be allocated to medium-need and high-need states and 25% of staff should be allocated low-need states. During off-peak season, 40-50% of staff should be allocated to high-need states and 50-60% of staff should be allocated to medium-need and low-need states.

Insights and Data Visualizations

I use PostgreSQL and Tableau to draw insights and answer key questions for Rockbuster's BI department. Here are some questions that were answered.

Which movies contributed the most/least to revenue gain?

Rockbuster's film database contains 1000 distinct films. Sports films contribute most to revenue while music films contribute least to revenue. However, this could be due to there being more sports films to rent and fewer music films to rent.

Which countries are Rockbuster customers based in?

Rockbuster's revenue come mostly from the Americas, Europe, and Asia. To increase revenue from the other regions, focus more of the marketing to the Carribean, Oceania, Middle East, and Africa regions.

Insights and Data Visualizations

There were several insights and visualizations made for this project. Here are some of them.

What are the busiest days of the week? What are the busiest hours of the day?

The busiest days of the week are Saturday and Sunday, and the slowest days of the week are Tuesday and Wednesday.

The busiest times of the day are 9:00AM to 4:00PM, and the slowest times of the day are from 10:00PM to 7:00AM.

How often do Instacart customers return to shop?

Instacart customers mostly return after 7 days or 30 days.

Customers were segmented based on how frequently they returned to shop. If a customer's median days since prior order is:

  • 10 or fewer: label the customer as a frequent customer
  • 20 or fewer but more than 10: label the customer as a regular customer
  • More than 20: label the customer as a non-frequent customer

With this segmentation, frequent customers typically return to shop in about 7 days, regular customers typically return to shop in about 14 days, and non-frequent customers return to shop in about 28 days.

Recommendations

Since the slowest days are Tuesday and Wednesday, the marketing team should focus their advertisements on Tuesday and Wednesday to increase more orders from their customers on these days. Avoid advertising between the hours of 11:00pm to 7:00am; otherwise advertising efforts will be missed by customers likely due to customers sleeping during these hours of the day.

If not already implemented, have a subscription service for customers who purchase goods on a weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly basis. For example, frequent customers would likely enjoy a weekly subscription for certain goods, regular customers would likely enjoy a biweekly subscription for certain goods, and non-frequent customers would likely enjoy a monthly subscription for certain goods.

Video Game Sales Analysis

Overview

GameCo, a new video game company, wants to use data to determine how to allocate the 2017 marketing budget across the regions to maximize return on investment.

Goal

Perform a descriptive analysis of a video game data set to better understand the behavior of video game sales figures across different geographic regions over time.

Purpose & Context

This project was part of my data analytics course at CareerFoundry to showcase the technical skills needed to analyze data using Excel and the soft skills needed to communicate insights.

Data Sets Used

Historical sales of video games (for games that sold more than 100,000 copies) spanning different platforms, genres, and publishing studios (Source: VGChartz)

Download Video Game Sales

Data Preparation and Analysis

  • Looked at the data set to understand the source, coverage, and relevance, ensuring it aligns with the business requirements.
  • Categorized the types of data, characteristics, and potential bias in the data set.
  • Removed duplicate records and removed columns not relevant for analysis.
  • Checked columns for missing values and either imputed the missing values or removed the records with missing values if imputing was not appropriate.
  • Corrected format inconsistencies and inaccuracies.
  • Used pivot tables to transform the data into something more usable and insightful.
  • Created groups so that similar items can be analyzed together.

  • Filtered the data to look at specific subsegments.
  • Created new variables to glean as many insights as possible.
  • Isolated the most interesting findings about the data.
  • Decided which type of analysis to pursue in response to business challenges.
  • Obtained descriptive statistics of video game sales across the regions to understand their statistical properties.

Insights and Data Visualizations

There were several insights and visualizations made for this project. Here are a couple of them.

How have sales figures performed across the various geographic regions over time?

GameCo assumed video game sales for the four various geographic regions have stayed the same over time. According to the data, however, it is clearly not that way at all. Sales (number of units sold) were increasing prior to 2008, and then sales began declining after 2008.

How has ratio of regional sales to global sales varied across the geographic regions over time?

To get a better idea on how to allocate the marketing budget for the year 2017, we looked at the historical proportion of sales for each region. Looking at the last 10 years (2007 - 2016), we can see that North America has been the leader in proportion of sales with a noticeable steady decline. Furthermore, we can the proportion of sales from Europe is steadily rising, eventually surpassing that of North America in 2016.

Recommendations

Allocate 32% of the marketing budget to North America at the start of 2017. Since proportion of sales for North America is declining, decrease allocation to 25% by the end of 2017.

Allocate 38% of the marketing budget to Europe at the start of 2017. Since proportion of sales for Europe is increasing, increase allocation to 45% by the end of 2017.

Allocate 19% of the marketing budget to Japan at the start of 2017. Since proportion of sales in Japan have declined overall, decrease allocation to 18% by the end of 2017.

Allocate 11% of the marketing budget to other regions at the start of 2017. Since proportion of sales for other regions is slowly rising, increase allocation to 12% by the end of 2017.
